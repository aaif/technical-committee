**Charter (the “Charter”) for the Technical Committee of the Agentic AI Foundation**

**Adopted March 3, 2026**

This Charter sets forth the purpose and operation of the Technical Committee (the “TC”) of the Agentic AI Foundation (“AAIF”), a directed fund of The Linux Foundation.   In the event of a conflict between the terms of the Charter and the terms of the AAIF charter or an action of the Governing Board of the AAIF (the “GB”), the AAIF charter will control. Capitalized terms used in this Charter but not defined have the meanings ascribed to them in the AAIF Charter.

1. **Purpose and Composition of the TC**

   1. The GB has established the TC with the purpose of assisting the GB with 1\) reviewing new project proposals, 2\) recommending projects that fall within the scope of and conform to the mission of AAIF, and 3\) applying the project lifecycle policy (the “Lifecycle Policy”) of AAIF to the Technical Projects.

   2. Composition.  Each Platinum Member of AAIF may appoint one representative to the TC (each, a “TC Representative”).

2. **Operations of the TC**

   1. The TC will implement and maintain, subject to initial GB approval, a Project Lifecycle Policy as well as future amendments thereto. 

   2. The TC may elect a TC Chair and, if desired, a Co-Chair, who will preside over TC meetings and serve until their resignation or replacement by the TC. 

   3. Responsibilities: The TC's role is to facilitate communication and collaboration between the Technical Projects and the broader open-source community.  The TC will be responsible for:

      1. establish, manage, and archive Working Groups enabling collaboration on matters in domains relevant to the scope of the AAIF;

      2. coordinating collaboration between Technical Projects, including the development of overall technical priorities and a vision for the community;

      3. recommending projects to the GB projects for acceptance as Technical Projects;

      4. deciding changes in a Technical Project’s status within the Lifecycle Policy;

      5. recommending resource priorities to the GB for supporting the AAIF’s Technical Projects;

      6. electing annually a chairperson, and co-chair if decided, to preside over meetings;

      7. applying and maintaining the Lifecycle Policy, subject to the approval of the Governing Board; and

      8. such other matters related to the technical role of the TC as may be communicated to the TC by the GB.

3. **TC Voting**

   1. While the TC aims to operate as a consensus-based community, if any decision requires a vote to move the business of the TC forward, the voting members of the TC will vote on a one\-vote\-per\-voting-member basis.

   2. Quorum for TC meetings requires at least fifty percent of the TC’s voting members to be present. The TC may continue to meet if a quorum is not met, but will be prevented from making any decisions requiring a vote at the meeting.

   3. Decisions can be made via lazy consensus. Lazy consensus is a "silence-as-agreement" model in which a proposal is automatically approved if no formal objection from a TC voting member is recorded within 72 hours. If an objection is raised, decisions made by electronic vote without a meeting require a majority vote of all TC voting members.

   4. If a vote cannot be resolved by the TC, any TC voting member may refer the matter to the GB for assistance in reaching a resolution.

4. **Amendments**

   1. This Charter may be amended at any time with the approval of two-thirds of the TC and the GB's approval, in accordance with the GB’s requirements.
